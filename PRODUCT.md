# Product

## Register

product

## Users

The primary users are university students preparing for job applications and school-side career advisors reviewing a student's readiness. They use the product in a focused planning workflow: upload a resume, optionally add a transcript and supporting files, then review recommended roles, a structured diagnosis, and a practical growth plan.

## Product Purpose

This product helps students understand the gap between their current evidence and recommended roles' capability requirements. Success means the student can see a clear ability profile, role-match score, capability gaps, learning path, development tasks, career advice, and targeted employment guidance without choosing a target role manually.

## Brand Personality

Precise, calm, and future-facing. The interface should feel like a light technical cockpit for career planning: fast, legible, quietly intelligent, and not promotional.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS dashboards, dark cyberpunk panels, loud purple gradients, decorative glass cards, large marketing hero sections, and card grids that repeat icon-heading-copy blocks. The experience should not feel like a brochure; it should feel like a diagnostic tool.

## Design Principles

Show diagnosis before explanation.
Make the required upload state unmissable, then progressively reveal the rest of the journey.
Treat scores as decision aids, not decoration.
Keep motion tied to unlock, progress, and result state changes.
Preserve the current backend boundary until dedicated upload and scoring APIs exist.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Support keyboard upload, visible focus states, readable contrast, reduced-motion alternatives, and color-independent score interpretation. Radar and score visuals must have text equivalents.
